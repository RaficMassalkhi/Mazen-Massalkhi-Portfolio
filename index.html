Functional Expertise

Strategic
Supply Chain
Planning

Supply Chain functional strategies must act as an enabler for achieving the organization's goals. It's essential to align with the organization's primary objectives and goals before establishing the Supply Chain Department's objectives, goals, strategies, and performance metrics.

Supply Chain
Digital
Transformation

Transitioning to advanced solutions like FnR, WMS, TMS, alongside harnessing AI, IoT, and Blockchain within supply chain technologies is critical. However, selecting the appropriate solution demands comprehensive due diligence, considering the organization's strategic direction and level of maturity.

Control Tower
& Data
Analytics

The challenge no longer resides in gathering data or executing complex analytical tasks but in identifying the crucial insights within data. Supply Chain KPIs must be carefully selected & synchronized with the organization’s KPIs and strategic objectives.

Supply Chain
Risk
Management

Like any business unit, Supply Chain must proactively identify, evaluate, and address both internal and external risks, particularly for global operations. It's vital to distinguish between known and unknown risks, implementing appropriate countermeasures for each.

Supply Chain
Sustainability
& SDGs

Supply Chain function plays a pivotal role in a company's sustainability efforts, with supply chain leaders often at the forefront of driving sustainability initiatives that support the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Logistics
Network
Design

Rent or buy?
In-house or outsource (3PL)?
Determining the ideal network configuration, capacity, and locations is crucial, requiring significant capital investments and strategic planning based on the organization's specific needs, limitations, and ambitions.

Distribution
Center
Design

A successful design requires a thorough understanding of the organization’s strategic direction, existing challenges, and customer service commitments. As DC Automation solutions evolve, it's recommended to evaluate the adoption of new technologies.

OpEx &
Process
Improvement

Pursuing the industry’s best practices in process improvement is fundamental to achieving operational excellence, ensuring an efficient and effective functioning of the supply chain.

Demand
Forecasting &
Replenishment

Striking the right balance between ensuring product availability and avoiding excess stock is crucial. This age-old challenge pits service levels against the cost of capital. Different industries and products require tailored forecasting methods. Not to forget the role of Data Base & inventory accuracy.

Global
Logistics
Management

Mastering global logistics is a complex art that involves choosing the correct Incoterms, selecting mode of transport and carriers, setting up upstream consolidation, determining the precise HS code, and adhering to export and import regulations to ensure swift market access and optimized landed cost.

E-Comm &
Omnichannel
Operations

E-commerce has enabled retailers to expand their reach. Nevertheless, this has also placed them in direct competition with international retailers, intensifying the supply chain competition on minimizing delivery times and maintaining efficiency.

Last Mile &
Customer
Service

The last mile delivery is crucial as it represents the final step in the customer's journey and must be executed flawlessly. The key to success in last-mile delivery lies in offering convenience, speed, reliability, visibility and flexibility.
